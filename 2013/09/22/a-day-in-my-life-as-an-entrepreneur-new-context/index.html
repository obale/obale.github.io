A Day in My Life as an Entrepreneur:
Sigimera Ltd. In Amsterdam

It is more than a month ago, I published the last blog post. Let me get you updated what happened during this time. There were two major, somehow interconnected, changes. The first is the move of myself to Amsterdam (the rest of the team stays in Austria and the company legal residence in the UK). The second is the decision to stop all marketing activities and get back to the drawing board. In this blog post I will try to explain to you why this decisions were made and what consequences they have.

Let me start with my move to Amsterdam. I will continue there with my Master studies at the VU Amsterdam, but that is only half of the truth. The other is that I will try to get in touch with the startup community. Not so much through startup events, but more directly with other entrepreneurs, at least for now. That was also the reason why I was in the last few weeks busy with the move and the restructuring of our communication means. As I have explained in one of my last blog post the team is the most crucial part, one reason more to continue with our internal meetings, nevertheless where we are. Until now that works pretty well… Actually better as all other video conferences before. The reason is the fast Internet connection and the updated equipment for this purpose.

The consequence for Sigimera Ltd. is that we have now an unofficial office in Amsterdam. One advantage is the direct connection to one of the biggest Internet Exchange point worldwide. Additional it extends our on-site impact to yet another country. One change was also the restructuring of our telephone system. Now we have a landline phone number in the Netherlands. This number can and will be redirect to all (or some) of our business partners, nevertheless where they are currently and without additional costs for the caller. This phone number allows us to travel around the world and get all calls without roaming costs. If necessary we will get other numbers in the countries where we are active. This is a big advantage for such a company as Sigimera Ltd. We have currently no official office and are spread across two to four countries (until now), nevertheless we are able to communicate in a cheap and reliable way with all stakeholders. Combined with other communication means, e.g. for weekly internal meetings, code quality and software planning platforms, this makes our company structure very flexible and lets us adapt to the ever changing market.

Now that we have a flexible company structure that allows us to communication across country borders and work together in collaborative way, it is time to focus on our business idea. The current plan is to go one or two steps back, stop the past marketing efforts for the Donation Service and build on top of this Donation Service a campaign management system. This extends not only our target market, but gives us also a little bit time to stabilize our company structure and maybe get new business partner. If you have worried in the past weeks if Sigimera is alive, I can you assure that it is and that more than ever.

As you can see we are no conventional company and are able to organize our structures on the go, as every startup should. Our flexibility and commitment to our ideas gives as good base for further development.

As always please let me know what you think. Do you face the same challenges? How do you solve them? What do you think about startup without office space? Looking forward to an interesting conversation and discussion.
